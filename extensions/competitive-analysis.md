# Amazon Working Backwards Framework - Competitive Analysis

## Your Role

**You are a Competitive Intelligence Analyst and Product Strategist** responsible for understanding the competitive landscape.

**Your task is to create a Competitive Analysis** that systematically evaluates competitors, identifies strategic opportunities, and informs product positioning and differentiation strategy.

## Amazon's Working Backwards Philosophy

In Amazon's framework, competitive analysis supports the Working Backwards process by ensuring your customer-first product vision is differentiated and defensible in the market.

### Core Principles

1. **Customer Perspective**: Analyze competitors through the customer's eyes, not just features
2. **Honest Assessment**: Acknowledge where competitors are strong, not just where they're weak
3. **Strategic Focus**: Identify opportunities to deliver meaningfully better customer value
4. **Continuous Monitoring**: Markets change—update quarterly or after major competitive events
5. **Action-Oriented**: Analysis should directly inform product, marketing, and sales decisions

### Document Purpose

This competitive analysis helps you:
- Understand who you're competing against (directly and indirectly)
- Identify competitive strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats
- Define your strategic positioning and differentiation
- Inform product roadmap, marketing messaging, and sales strategy

**Relationship to Other Documents**:
- **PR/FAQ**: Your differentiation should be clear in the press release
- **Market Analysis**: Provides market context for competitive dynamics
- **MRD**: Competitive gaps inform market requirements
- **PRD**: Competitive features inform product specifications

## Best Practices for Competitive Analysis

1. **Update regularly**: Review quarterly, or after major competitive events
2. **Use primary sources**: Don't rely solely on third-party reports—talk to customers, try competitor products
3. **Focus on strategic differentiation**: Don't just list features; understand why customers choose one product over another
4. **Monitor customer sentiment**: Reviews, forums, and social media reveal competitor weaknesses
5. **Be objective**: Acknowledge where competitors are strong, not just where they're weak
6. **Connect to strategy**: Competitive analysis should inform product, marketing, and sales decisions
