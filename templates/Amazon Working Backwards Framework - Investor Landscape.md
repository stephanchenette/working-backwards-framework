# Amazon Working Backwards Framework - Investor Landscape

## Your Role

**You are a Venture Capital Analyst and Fundraising Strategist** responsible for mapping the investor ecosystem and developing fundraising strategy.

**Your task is to create an Investor Landscape document** that identifies potential investors, analyzes their investment criteria, benchmarks valuations, and develops a staged fundraising roadmap for your company.

## Amazon's Working Backwards Philosophy

In Amazon's framework, investor landscape analysis is a **strategic planning document** that supports long-term capital strategy. While not part of the core Working Backwards process, it ensures you have the capital to execute on the customer vision defined in your PR/FAQ.

### Core Principles

1. **Customer-First Fundraising**: Raise capital to serve customers better, not for vanity metrics
2. **Strategic Capital**: Choose investors who add strategic value beyond money
3. **Stage-Appropriate Targeting**: Different investors for different stages—don't waste time on wrong-stage VCs
4. **Relationship Building**: Fundraising starts years before you need capital
5. **Data-Driven Targeting**: Research investor portfolios, thesis, and track record

### Document Purpose

This investor landscape analysis helps you:
- Identify investors aligned with your stage, sector, and geography
- Understand what investors look for in your category
- Benchmark valuations and funding amounts by stage
- Develop a sequenced fundraising strategy
- Build relationships with investors before you need capital

**Relationship to Other Documents**:
- **PR/FAQ**: Your customer vision attracts investors
- **Market Analysis**: TAM/SAM validates market opportunity for investors
- **MRD**: Market traction proves investment thesis
- **M&A Analysis**: Exit potential affects investor interest

### Expected Outcome

A strategic fundraising roadmap that includes:
- Prioritized investor targets by stage (seed, A, B, etc.)
- Investment criteria and proof points required
- Valuation benchmarks and target raise amounts
- Relationship cultivation timeline
- Pitch strategy and sequencing

## 9. Pitch Strategy & Positioning

### 9.1 Investor Pitch Framework

**Slide 1: Problem** (30 seconds)
* What customer pain are you solving?
* How big is the problem? (market size)

**Slide 2: Solution** (30 seconds)
* Your product/service in simple terms
* How it solves the problem

**Slide 3: Market Opportunity** (1 minute)
* TAM/SAM/SOM
* Why now? Market timing

**Slide 4: Product Demo** (2 minutes)
* Show, don't tell
* Customer testimonial or case study

**Slide 5: Business Model** (1 minute)
* How you make money
* Unit economics

**Slide 6: Traction** (1 minute)
* Revenue growth
* Customer metrics
* Key milestones achieved

**Slide 7: Competition** (30 seconds)
* Competitive landscape
* Your differentiation

**Slide 8: Team** (30 seconds)
* Founding team credentials
* Key hires

**Slide 9: Financials** (1 minute)
* Historical revenue
* Projections (3 years)

**Slide 10: The Ask** (30 seconds)
* How much you're raising
* Use of funds
* Key milestones you'll hit

**Total Time**: 8-10 minutes (leave 20+ minutes for Q&A)

### 9.2 Common Investor Objections & Responses

### D. Fundraising Best Practices

1. **Always be fundraising**: Build relationships before you need capital
2. **Raise proactively**: Don't wait until you have <6 months runway
3. **Choose investors carefully**: Look for strategic value, not just money
4. **Negotiate from strength**: Multiple term sheets = better terms
5. **Move quickly**: Fundraising should take 3-6 months, not 12
6. **Be transparent**: Hiding problems makes them worse
7. **Follow up promptly**: Respond to investor questions within 24 hours
8. **Leverage competition**: Create FOMO by running a tight process
9. **Get warm intros**: Cold emails rarely work
